Mission Statement

Health Hack is a hackathon run by Open Knowledge Australia (@OKFNau). It was started in 2013 by Maia Sauren as a sister hackathon to Open Knowledge’s Govhack event.


Where Govhack was all about getting some usage out of newly available open government datasets (the primary goals of the Open Knowledge organisation) Health Hack is about helping Medical Researchers solve their problems.


Putting Software Developers and Designers in the same room as Medical Researchers leads to new innovative solutions to problems that would otherwise not be discovered. People from different backgrounds (E.g. Biologists and Computer Scientists) won’t normally have a chance to talk to each other and will have different ways of looking at problems. Getting different types of people together can lead to cross pollination of ideas.


Creating something over a weekend can have a great impact. For researchers, a simple proof of concept can help a grant application. For hackers, a successful hack is the best way to get your foot in the door into the industry.
